⚠️ Risk Disclaimer

IMPORTANT - PLEASE READ CAREFULLY BEFORE INVESTING

All investments carry significant risk, including potential loss of your entire principal investment. Past performance does not guarantee future results. Carefully review this entire disclaimer before investing.

1. General Investment Risk

Investing in financial securities involves substantial risk of loss. The value of your investment may fluctuate significantly based on market conditions, economic factors, political events, and other variables beyond our control.

Bluepeak Financial cannot guarantee that your investments will achieve positive returns. You may lose some or all of your invested capital.

2. Market Risk

The financial markets can experience significant volatility. Stock prices fall, bond yields rise, currencies fluctuate, and economic cycles occur unpredictably. During market downturns, your portfolio value may decline substantially.

  • Equity markets can decline 20%, 30%, or more in short periods
  • Fixed income securities face interest rate and credit risks
  • Emerging markets experience higher volatility than developed markets
  • Economic recessions can severely impact investment returns

3. Concentration Risk

Despite our diversification efforts, concentrated exposure in certain sectors, regions, or asset classes can amplify losses during adverse market conditions. Diversification does not eliminate investment risk.

4. Liquidity Risk

Some investments may be difficult or impossible to sell quickly at fair prices. During market stress or crises, liquidity can evaporate rapidly, potentially forcing sales at significant losses.

5. Credit Risk

When we invest in bonds and fixed-income securities, there is risk that the issuer will default and fail to pay interest or principal. Even highly-rated securities can experience defaults.

6. Interest Rate Risk

Bond prices fall when interest rates rise. If we invest significant portions of your portfolio in bonds, and interest rates increase sharply, the value of those bonds will decline.

7. Currency Risk

International investments expose you to currency fluctuations. Adverse foreign exchange movements can significantly impact returns, even if the underlying investment performs well.

8. Geopolitical & Macro Risk

Wars, terrorism, political instability, trade disputes, pandemics, natural disasters, and other global events can severely disrupt financial markets and investment returns. These events are often unpredictable.

9. No Performance Guarantees

There are absolutely no guarantees regarding investment performance. Bluepeak Financial's historical returns and projected returns are not promises of future performance.

  • Historical returns may not repeat
  • Average returns cited may not reflect individual client experience
  • Projected returns are estimates, not guarantees
  • Market conditions can change dramatically and unexpectedly

10. Management Risk

Investment decisions made by our portfolio managers may underperform the market. Even skilled managers cannot consistently beat market benchmarks, particularly after fees.

Our investment strategies may fail to achieve intended objectives. Market changes may render our approaches less effective.

11. Inflation Risk

If investment returns do not keep pace with inflation, you will lose purchasing power even if your account value appears stable. Inflation can erode real returns over time.

12. Psychological Risk

During market downturns, investors often sell at the worst possible time, locking in losses. We encourage long-term investing, but we cannot prevent you from making poor emotional decisions.

13. Regulatory & Tax Risk

Changes in tax laws, regulations, or government policies can negatively impact investment returns. We cannot predict or control regulatory changes.

Tax implications of your investments are your responsibility. Consult a tax professional regarding tax consequences.

14. Operational & Custody Risk

While we employ industry-standard safeguards, cyber attacks, system failures, or other operational issues could theoretically impact your funds. Custodians are regulated, but no system is fail-proof.

15. Company Risk

While Bluepeak Financial is a well-established firm, there is always business risk. In the unlikely event of company insolvency, your funds are held with independent custodians and remain protected, but service could be disrupted.

16. Suitability Considerations

Our investment plans may not be suitable for all investors. You should consider:

  • Your age and investment time horizon
  • Your financial situation and income stability
  • Your risk tolerance and emotional capacity to handle losses
  • Your financial goals and obligations
  • Whether you can afford to hold investments through downturns

Only invest money you can afford to lose completely.

17. Minimum Investment Risk

Our minimum investments ($5,000-$100,000) should represent money you don't need for living expenses or emergency reserves. Tying up capital in investments limits your financial flexibility.

18. Limited Historical Data

While Bluepeak Financial has 15+ years of experience, this represents only a few market cycles. Past performance during certain economic periods may not predict performance in different conditions.

19. Realistic Return Expectations

Bluepeak Financial does not promise or guarantee positive returns. Our average historical returns are:

  • Basic Plan: 8-10% average annual return (historical average, NOT guaranteed)
  • Standard Plan: 11-13% average annual return (historical average, NOT guaranteed)
  • Premium Plan: 12-15% average annual return (historical average, NOT guaranteed)

These figures represent historical averages. Actual returns will vary year to year and may be significantly lower or negative in poor market conditions.

20. Due Diligence is Your Responsibility

Before investing, you should:

  • Thoroughly understand our investment approach and risks
  • Review our fees and compare them to other investment options
  • Consider seeking advice from your own financial or legal advisor
  • Verify our credentials and regulatory status independently
  • Read all disclosures and terms carefully
  • Ask questions until you fully understand the risks
